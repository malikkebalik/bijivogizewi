Most Innovative Gaming, Work 2023 Monitors

MONITORS – Here are the most innovative monitors of 2023. These monitors pushed boundaries in a manner unmatched by any other displays this year.

This year marked a significant breakthrough in the realm of monitors. In 2023, we witnessed the debut of OLED gaming monitors, refresh rates that surpassed our wildest expectations, and innovative designs that significantly improved productivity and media enjoyment.

While the tried-and-true classics remain on our list of top monitors for those seeking reliability, the monitors introduced this year truly pushed the boundaries in a manner unparalleled by any other displays.

Most innovative: Samsung Odyssey OLED G9

Innovation in monitors goes beyond sheer size, and the Odyssey OLED G9 exemplifies true innovation. Boasting an impressive 32:9 display spanning 49 inches diagonally, Samsung has consistently championed this form factor. However, what sets the Odyssey OLED G9 apart this year is its utilization of a QD-OLED panel, marking a significant advancement.

While OLED technology itself is visually stunning, the practicality of its implementation on the Odyssey OLED G9 is noteworthy. In contrast to LCD monitors, which require space for a backlight, OLED is remarkably thin. This thin profile enabled Samsung to substantially reduce the size of the Odyssey OLED G9, presenting a sleek metal frame and a more understated curve compared to its predecessor. Although both monitors are substantial in size, the previous Odyssey Neo G9’s aggressive curve and bulky plastic back made it impractical for most users.

The panel transition also elevates the monitor’s image quality significantly. While the Odyssey Neo G9 offered impressive visuals for an LCD monitor, the perfect black levels achieved by OLED enhance the immersive experience, especially in cinematic games. Coupled with the 32:9 aspect ratio, the Odyssey OLED G9 immerses users in the gaming world, delivering the most captivating gaming experience to date.

While the Odyssey OLED G9 might not introduce a completely new and unconventional concept like the Samsung Odyssey Ark, it takes an already exceptional foundation and enhances it through a thoughtful panel upgrade, making it the most innovative monitor of the year.

Runner-up: Alienware 500Hz gaming monitor

This year marked a significant stride in the realm of high-speed gaming monitors. Alienware led the way by unveiling a groundbreaking 500Hz display, a substantial leap forward in the competitive gaming arena.

With a refresh rate of 500Hz, the screen updates every 2 milliseconds, presenting a notable advantage in certain games. Titles such as Counter-Strike 2, Valorant, or Rainbow Six Siege benefit from the high frame rate achievable with a 500Hz display. Notably, Alienware integrated an Nvidia Reflex module into the monitor, enabling users to assess their latency from the moment they click.

Alienware isn’t the sole player in this domain. Asus also introduced a 540Hz display this year, providing additional choices for competitive gamers. However, it’s essential to note that while both monitors push the boundaries, they are confined to 1080p resolution with moderate image quality, making them more suitable for those specifically focused on competitive gaming rather than a broader audience.

Honorable mention: Asus ROG Swift PG27AQDM

The Asus ROG Swift PG27AQDM may not be the first 16:9 OLED gaming monitor, but it stands as the premier choice. Since the advent of OLED gaming monitors, there has been a clamor among PC gamers for a conventional 16:9 display in a reasonable size, and the PG27AQDM fulfills that demand perfectly. Boasting a 27-inch OLED screen, 1440p resolution, and an impressive 240Hz refresh rate, these specifications strike an unexpectedly ideal balance for high-end PC gaming.

OLED monitors have traditionally faced challenges with brightness, but Asus ingeniously addresses this concern with the PG27AQDM. Moreover, users can still enjoy the impeccable black levels and boundless contrast inherent to OLED technology.

The most noteworthy advancement in the monitor landscape this year has been the integration of OLED, and the Asus ROG Swift PG27AQDM epitomizes this innovation. While alternatives like LG’s UltraGear OLED 27 utilize the same panel, in our assessment, Asus emerges as the preferred choice.
