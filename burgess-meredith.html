Burgess Meredith

Publish date: 2024-07-04

Burgess Meredith was an American actor who rose to prominence after playing George Milton in the 1939 film Of Mice and Men. He was also a director, producer, and writer.

Where was Burgess Meredith born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Burgess Meredith was born on November 16, 1907, in Cleveland, Ohio, to Ida Beth Burgess and William George Meredith. He was an American by nationality, but he had mixed ancestors. Burgess attended Hoosac High School and graduated in 1926. He went to Amherst College before working as a reporter for the Stamford Advocate.

How did Burgess Meredith start his Professional Career?

Meredith served in the United States Army Air Forces during WWII. He eventually advanced to the rank of captain and was discharged in 1945. The late actor then began working in the theater. He was a member of the Civic Repertory Theatre in New York. He not only performed in plays such as Winterset and Romeo and Juliet, but he also directed a number of them.

Later, he made his big-screen debut as an uncredited extra in the 1935 film The Scoundrel. He established himself as a leading man in Hollywood after starring in Of Mice and Men alongside Betty Field, Lon Chaney Jr., Charles Bickford, and Noah Beery Jr.

Burgess’s credits include The Story of G.I. Joe, A Walk in the Sun, The Twilight Zone, Batman, the Rock film series, The Day of the Locust, Foul Play, Clash of the Titans, and many more. He played Grandpa Gustafson in the 1995 romantic comedy film Grumpier Old Men, alongside Jack Lemmon, Walter Matthau, Ann-Margret, Sophia Loren, Kevin Pollak, and Daryl Hannah.

Burgess Meredith’s death

Burgess died at the age of 89. He died in his Malibu home on September 9, 1997, and his body was cremated. His death was caused by Alzheimer’s disease and melanoma complications.
