Publish date: 2024-08-01

Who is Lucy Verasamy?

Lucy Verasamy is an English Weather Forecaster who is popular as a weather forecaster for SKY.

She also worked for ITV Breakfast as a weather forecaster and environment correspondent.

Currently, she works as a forecaster for ITV Weather, ITV Racing, and ITV’s Good Morning Britain.

She has also been titled the hottest weather woman.

Previously, Lucy Verasamy worked with Sky News and Meteorologist with Press Association & MeteoGroup: Europe’s leading private weather company.

Lucy Verasamy: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Lucy Verasamy was born on 2 August 1980 to English parents in King’s Lynn, Norfolk, England, United Kingdom.

Her current age as of 2023 is 42 Her ethnicity is English.

Lucy Verasamy spent her early life in the same place where she was born. Information on her father and mother is unknown.

Furthermore, she has one sibling, her younger sister.

In which school did Lucy Verasamy attend?

Lucy Verasamy had a sheer interest in pursuing a career as a Weather Forecaster.

So, for the sake of her fostering interest in Geography, she completed her BSc (Hons) degree in Geography from Brunel University in 2001.

Before joining Brunel University, she attended three different schools.

Those schools were Silfield Primary School in King’s Lynn, Norfolk, Framlingham College Junior School in Suffolk, and King Edward VII School in King’s Lynn.

From these schools, she graduated studying ‘A-Level Geography’.

Thus, Lucy Verasamy established a strong foundation to pursue a career as a Weather Forecaster.

Lucy Verasamy: Professional Life, Career

Lucy Verasamy initiated her career after her graduation from Brunel University in 2001 as a forecaster at Press Association’s weather center.

There, she worked for four years as a forecaster who forecasts newspapers and scripts for radio. Then, she tried to make a positive move towards a big achievement by joining Sky Weather Centre.

Later, she moved to SKY and worked as a weather forecaster on the ‘Sky News breakfast show Sunrise.

After working for nearly three years, she joined ITV Breakfast on 6 September 2010, as a weather forecaster and environment correspondent in the newly launched program Daybreak.

Lucy Verasamy left the program on 2 August 2012. Since then, she has been working on the national weather forecasts for ITV.

Furthermore, she works as a weather expert for the ITV Racing Team.

 In addition, she also works as a relief weather presenter for Good Morning Britain. 

In 2019, she worked the promotions for the M&S Fresh Market.

Verasamy was set to co-host the new ITV travel show How to Spend It Well on Holiday with Phillip Schofield in March 2020.

The show was canceled because of the COVID-19 pandemic, but it was planned to be resurrected at a later date.

Lucy joined colleagues ITV weather presenters Laura Tobin and Alex Beresford on the digital series Climate Crisis: Our Changing World in November 2020.

Lucy Verasamy starred in Marks & Spencer’s “Fresh Market Buffet” advertising campaign in 2021, which featured grilled produce from Marks & Spencer’s growers and producers from North Scotland to Jersey.

Social Work

Lucy Verasamy has also worked for social causes. She worked as a fundraiser for the Give Hunger the Boot campaign in 2014.
