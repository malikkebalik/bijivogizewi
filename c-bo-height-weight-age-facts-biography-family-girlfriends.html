C-Bo Facts

  • On April 20, 1993, he released his debut album Gas Chamber and it included songs like Bald Head Nut, Chronic Conference, Gas Chamber, Shots Out, Survival In the Garden, Realer Than Real, and Liquor Sto. The album peaked at #53 on the U.S. Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.
  • C-Bo’s 4th studio album Til My Casket Drops was released on February 24, 1998, and it peaked at #4 on the U.S. Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart and at #41 on the U.S. Billboard 200 chart. Some of the tracks from the album are Ride Til’ We Die (ft. 151 and Mob Figaz), Deadly Game, Desperado Outlaws, Til My Casket Drop, Raised In Hell (ft. Big Syke), and Can We All Ball (ft. Killa Tay and JT the Bigga Figga).
  • C-Bo became one of the first rappers to be jailed due to his lyrical content because he had criticized political officials and police on his track Deadly Game.
