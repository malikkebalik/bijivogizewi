Maria Sakkari – Professional Career

Her parents introduced her to Tennis at the age of six, and she has had only one aim in her heart, mind, and soul ever then: to become a successful Tennis player. At the age of 18, she relocated to Barcelona for proper training.

She was defeated in her first two WTA Tour matches in 2015. Maria lost in the first round of qualification at the Rio Open to Maria Irigoyen, and she also fell in the last round of qualification at the Bucharest Open to Daria Kasatkina. She was always prepared to confront failures, yet she never gave up.

She took it all in stride and continued to try. Following the US Open, she broke into the top 200 for the first time, landing at 185. Later that year, she qualified for the Australian Open, where she defeated Wang Yafan.

In the same year, she qualified but was defeated in the first round by Irina-Camella Begu. She made her first WTA Tour semi-final in 2017 by defeating Caroline Wozniacki, Elena Vesnina, and Alize Cornet at the Wuhan Open. In October 2017, her achievement propels her into the top 50 in the WTA rankings.

It lasted more than 3 hours, and she won in the second lag but lost in the end. Following that, she competed in Wimbledon but was unable to advance since she was eliminated in the second round.
