Early Life

Helen Lydia Mironoff, the daughter of a Russian father and an English mother, was born at Queen Charlotte’s and Chelsea Hospital in London’s Hammersmith neighborhood on July 26, 1945. Her father was born into an exiled Russian nobility family who are aristocrats and migrated to England with his father when he was 2 years old.

She was the second of three children; she had a younger brother named Peter Basil and an older sister named Katherine.

Meanwhile, in 1951, her father changed the family name to Mirren by deed poll.

Furthermore, Mirren attended St. Bernard’s High School for Girls in Southend-on-Sea and Hamlet Court Primary School in Westcliff-on-Sea, where she performed in school productions of Hansel and Gretel and had the lead role. Additionally, she then enrolled in the New College of Speech and Drama, a teaching institution in London. When she was 20, she played Cleopatra in the National Youth Theatre’s production of Antony and Cleopatra at the Old Vic. She credits this role with “launching my career” and getting her signed with agent Al Parker.

In the late 1960s, Mirren’s work with the NYT led to her signing with agent Al Parker and receiving an invitation to join the Royal Shakespeare Company. Mirren participated in roughly a dozen significant theater productions between 1966 and 1971.

In the 1969 movie “Age of Consent,” Helen Mirren portrayed the muse of a middle-aged artist. Her on-screen career grew soon. She was on the British television series “Prime Suspect” as Jane Tennison for seven seasons from 1991 to 2006.

After making her Broadway debut in 1994, Mirren received two Tony Award nominations for Best Actress for her roles in “A Month in the Country” in 1995 and “Dance of Death” in 2002, both of which she co-starred in.
