Meet Hideki Matsuyama Caddie- Shota Hayafuji Salary And Job Details Explored

Publish date: 2024-07-06

In this article, you'll discover interesting details about Shota Hayafuji, the caddie of Hideki Matsuyama, a Japanese professional golfer. 

Hideki Matsuyama, the former world No. 2, has won the Memorial Tournament, two World Golf Championship tournaments, and two Waste Management Phoenix Opens so far in his career. Of course, his most significant achievement was at the 2021 Masters competition.

But, on all of these occasions, who is carrying his bag?

Meet Shota Hayafuji, Hideki Matsuyama's caddie. 

Hideki Matsuyama Caddie: Who Is Shota Hayafuji?

Hideki has had the same man in his bag for the past few years. Shota Hayafuji is his name, and he appears to be a very good player himself, as evidenced by his China Tour player profile.

Shota Hayafuji Age: How Old Is He? 

Hideki Matsuyama's caddie, Shota Hayafuji, appears in his twenties. We could not give our readers his precise age because he has made his age information public. 

His boss, Hideki Matsuyama, is 29 years old. On February 25, 1992, he was born in Matsuyama, Ehime, Japan. 

Shota Hayafuji Biography

Shota first appeared with the six-time PGA Tour winner at the Sony Open in Hawaii and has been with him ever since.

Hayafuji assisted Hideki in claiming The Master's title earlier this year, making him the first Japanese player.

It's worth noting that Hayafuji competes in golf on the China Tour.

He shot a five-over 77 in the last round, which included four birdies, a quadruple bogey, a double, a triple, and, ultimately, a hole-in-one.

Shota Hayafuji Job 

A player and a caddy usually work together for years. Hayafuji has been collaborating with Matsuyama since late 2018.

Shota Hayafuji Salary Details 

Hayafuji's pay is determined by Matsuyama's performance in each tournament, as it is with other caddies. Every caddie has a wage structure that they agree on with the golfer with whom they work. This information is usually kept private.

According to the PGA Tour, Matsuyama has earned $1.6 million in the 2021 season thus far. Depending on where they place on the leaderboard, caddies often earn five to ten percent of the golfer's earnings.

Hayafuji's cut would be between $80,000 and $160,000. Given Matsuyama's performance in the overall competitions, this number is expected to skyrocket.
