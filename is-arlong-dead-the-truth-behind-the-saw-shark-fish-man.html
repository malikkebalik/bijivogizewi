Is Arlong Dead? The Truth Behind the Saw-Shark Fish-Man

Publish date: 2024-07-15

Arlong is one of the most notorious villains in the One Piece series, and the main antagonist of the Arlong Park Arc. He is a saw-shark fish-man who was the captain of the Arlong Pirates, a former member of the Sun Pirates, and the older half-brother of Madam Shyarly. Arlong’s Past and Crimes

Arlong was born in the Fish-Man District of Fish-Man Island, where he grew up with a hatred for humans due to the oppression and discrimination they faced from them. He joined the Sun Pirates, a crew of fish-men and mermen led by Fisher Tiger, who fought against the World Government and freed many slaves. Arlong was one of the most violent and ruthless members of the crew, and often clashed with Jinbe, the first mate, who advocated for peaceful coexistence with humans

After Fisher Tiger’s death, Arlong was captured and sent to Impel Down, the underwater prison of the World Government. However, he was released thanks to Jinbe becoming one of the Seven Warlords of the Sea, a group of pirates who work for the government. Arlong then split from Jinbe’s crew and formed his own crew, the Arlong Pirates, with the goal of conquering the East Blue, the weakest of the four seas. He believed that fish-men were superior to humans and treated them as inferior beings. He also had a tattoo of the Sun Pirates’ symbol on his chest, which he later modified to hide the sun and emphasize the crossbones

Arlong and his crew settled on the Conomi Islands, where they terrorized the local population and extorted money from them. He also killed Bell-mère, the foster mother of Nami and Nojiko, and forced Nami to join his crew as a cartographer, taking advantage of her skills to map the entire world. He also made a deal with her, promising to free her and her village if she paid him 100 million berries. However, he had no intention of keeping his promise and planned to kill her once she completed her task. He also built Arlong Park, a fortress that served as his base and symbol of his power

Arlong’s Defeat and Arrest

Arlong’s reign of terror came to an end when he encountered the Straw Hat Pirates, a crew of young pirates led by Monkey D. Luffy, who came to the island in search of Nami. Luffy, who considered Nami as his friend and navigator, decided to fight Arlong and free her from his clutches. He was joined by his crewmates Zoro, Usopp, and Sanji, as well as Johnny, Yosaku, and the villagers of Cocoyasi Village, who rebelled against Arlong’s tyranny

Arlong proved to be a formidable opponent, as he was physically stronger and faster than most humans, and could use his saw-shaped nose and teeth as weapons. He also had a weapon called Kiribachi, a six-bladed sword that resembled a saw. He also had a deep knowledge of fish-men anatomy, and could exploit the weaknesses of his opponents. He also had a loyal and powerful crew, composed of Hatchan, a six-armed octopus fish-man, Kuroobi, a ray fish-man, Chew, a sea cow fish-man, and several other fish-men and mermen

However, Arlong and his crew were ultimately defeated by the Straw Hats and their allies, who displayed remarkable courage and determination. Luffy, who was enraged by Arlong’s cruelty and betrayal of Nami, fought him in a fierce battle that destroyed most of Arlong Park. Luffy managed to overcome Arlong’s strength and speed with his rubber powers and his willpower, and delivered the final blow with his signature attack, Gum-Gum Bazooka, smashing Arlong’s head through the stone tower of his base

With Arlong’s defeat, Nami was freed from his control and officially joined the Straw Hat Pirates as their navigator. The villagers celebrated their liberation and thanked the Straw Hats for their help. Arlong and his crew were then arrested by Commodore Pudding Pudding, a marine officer who arrived at the scene after being informed by Nezumi, a corrupt marine captain who had been working with Arlong. Arlong was last seen being dragged away by the marines, cursing Luffy and Nami
