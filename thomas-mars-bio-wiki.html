Who is Thomas Mars?

Thomas Mars is one of a popular French musicians. He is a founder and the lead singer of the French alternative rock band, Phoenix.

The band also won a Grammy with “Wolfgang Amadeus Phoenix” in 2010.

Thomas Mars: Career, Professional life

Thomas Mars created his band at a young age with his friends. They firstly pressed their first 500 singles on their own label before being signed to Paris-based label, Source Records. Up to now, the band has released six albums and also won a Grammy Award in 2010.

Thomas was on the vocals, Deck d’Arcy was a bassist, and Chris and Laurent on the guitar and worked together to create the band ‘Phoenix‘. The band also gained much popularity. They released its initial singles, ‘Heatwave’ and ‘Party Time,’ in 1999.

Thomas then produced and wrote the single ‘Too Young,’ in 2000. It was their first single to enter the charts. It peaked at Number 97 in France and at Number 148 in the UK. The song was also later included in the romantic comedy films ‘Shallow Hal’ (2001) and ‘Lost in Translation’ (2003). It was also performed in an episode of ‘Saturday Night Live.’

Singles ‘Party Time’ and ‘Too Young,’ sold 150,000 copies worldwide. Likely, their second album, ‘Alphabetical,’ was released on March 22, 2004. Following ‘Alphabetical,’ was released where Thomass and his bandmates toured three continents for it. They released the live album ‘Live! Thirty Days Ago’ only 30 days after the tour ended.

The fourth album, ‘Wolfgang Amadeus Phoenix,’ was released on May 25, 2009. Phoenix then received Grammy Award’ (for the ‘Best Alternative Music Album’). The lead single of the album, ‘1901,’ was a tribute to early Paris and was called as the band’s biggest success to date.

The band’s launched their fifth studio album Bankrupt in the year 2013. Lately, the Phoenix band was also featured in a NetFlix series, A Very Murray Christmas. It was directed by Thomas’s wife, Sofia Coppola.
