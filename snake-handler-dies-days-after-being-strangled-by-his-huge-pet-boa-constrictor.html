Snake handler dies days after being strangled by his huge pet boa constrictor

Publish date: 2024-09-24

The tragic incident involving a snake handler and his pet boa constrictor has resulted in the handler’s untimely death. Elliot Senseman, 27, from Fogelsville, Pennsylvania, USA, passed away after his 18-foot-long boa constrictor wrapped around his neck, leading to accidental asphyxiation and an anoxic brain injury.

Police responded to a call from Senseman’s family member on July 20, finding him unresponsive with the massive snake constricting his body. In an attempt to free him, the officers had to resort to shooting the boa constrictor, which unfortunately did not die instantly but eventually succumbed after slithering away and getting euthanized.

Senseman’s mother, Heather, expressed her grief and emphasized her son’s experience in handling snakes, mentioning his efforts in rescuing and caring for neglected reptiles. She also emphasized his commitment to providing proper habitats for the snakes, mirroring their natural environments as closely as possible.

The tragic incident has brought to light the potential risks associated with keeping large and aggressive snakes as pets, prompting a reflection on the responsibilities and challenges of handling such animals.
