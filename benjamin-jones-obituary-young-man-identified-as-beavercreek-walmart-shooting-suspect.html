Benjamin Jones obituary: Young man identified as Beavercreek Walmart shooting suspect

Publish date: 2024-09-18

In a shocking incident, the Beavercreek Police Department has identified 20-year-old Benjamin Jones as the shooting suspect in the Beavercreek Walmart shooting. The tragic event unfolded on Monday night, with Jones entering the store and opening fire, injuring four individuals.

According to the latest update from the City of Beavercreek Police Department, three females and one male were among the victims, all of whom sustained injuries. Three victims are in critical condition, while the fourth has non-life-threatening injuries. Regrettably, the shooter, identified as Jones, succumbed to an apparent self-inflicted gunshot wound.

The Beavercreek Police Department is actively collecting evidence and information related to the incident. A press conference is scheduled for this afternoon, during which additional details will be released to the public. The motive behind the shooting remains unclear at this time.

The harrowing incident occurred at the Walmart located at 3360 Pentagon Blvd. around 8:35 p.m. on Monday, November 20, as reported by Beavercreek Police Department Capt. Scott Molnar. The police have yet to disclose the specific nature of the victims’ injuries.

In response to the tragic event, Walmart issued a statement expressing deep sorrow: “We’re heartbroken by what’s happened at our Beavercreek, Ohio store. This remains a developing situation, and we’re working closely with investigators on the scene.”

As the community grapples with the aftermath of this senseless act of violence, authorities are diligently working to provide clarity on the circumstances surrounding the shooting. The press conference promises to shed light on critical details as the investigation unfolds.
